About Us

It's been awhile since we've been able to get out and explore. I love the Northwest but this winter was a bit much! We have one bit of sad information to pass along. Our favorite italian restaurant, Iorio has closed. There was a sad sigh that floated over the city that day. We wish them well and if they ever decide to come back we'll be waiting!
Now for some good news! We did get out one rainy Friday afternoon and found, what has to be, one of the best chocolatiers in Portland!

A Yen For Chocolates

This is Cristina Yen, the chocolatier at A Yen for Chocolates. Her shop is cozy and overflowing with beautiful chocolates. We tried a few (ok, more than a few!). Her chocolates are beautiful as is the packaging that she uses. However what will stop you in your tracks are her flavors and the way she combines them. My favorites were the white chocolate-lemon grass-basil and the salted caramel with brandy and cardamom, both of which are indescribably wonderful.

We asked her how she came up with such unique flavors. "Chocolate is comforting, romantic, and sensual. I look to multiple sources of inspiration -- the changing seasons, the emotional and cultural tones of various holidays and celebrations -- so that I can create chocolates that evoke these feelings and experiences for you." She does it fabulously and you owe it to yourself to see her and taste for yourself. Her shop itself is only open on Fridays but you can place orders on her website. Don't miss this talented chocolatier!

Cristina uses only the finest ingredients & some of the most amazing flavor combinations you've ever tried!

Her chocolates are as beautiful as they are lucious.

Her packing is creative, simple and elegant. With her chocolates lovingly packed inside this would be the perfect gift.
